## Call Handling Framework

### Opening

**Standard Greeting:**
> "Good [morning/afternoon/evening], thank you for calling SnowTelco. My name is [Name]. How may I help you today?"

**Security Verification:**
1. Account number or mobile number
2. Full name on account
3. Security question OR
4. Last 4 digits of payment card OR
5. Postcode

### LISTEN Framework

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **L** - Listen | Let customer explain fully without interruption |
| **I** - Investigate | Ask clarifying questions |
| **S** - Solve | Provide solution or workaround |
| **T** - Tell | Explain what you've done |
| **E** - Ensure | Confirm customer satisfaction |
| **N** - Next Steps | Explain any follow-up actions |

### Closing

> "Is there anything else I can help you with today? Thank you for calling SnowTelco. Have a great day!"

## Common Enquiry Types

### Billing Enquiries

**"My bill is higher than expected"**

1. Verify account and review current bill
2. Compare with previous bills
3. Check for:
   - Out-of-bundle usage (data, calls, SMS)
   - Roaming charges
   - Premium rate calls/texts
   - One-off charges
4. Explain charges clearly
5. Offer bill breakdown by email
6. If error found, process credit

**Credit Authority:**
| Agent Level | Credit Limit |
|-------------|--------------|
| Standard | Up to £25 |
| Senior | Up to £100 |
| Team Lead | Up to £500 |
| Manager | Unlimited |

### Technical Support

**"I have no signal"**

1. Confirm device and location
2. Check for known outages in area
3. Troubleshooting steps:
   - Restart device
   - Toggle airplane mode
   - Check SIM seated correctly
   - Try manual network selection
4. If unresolved, raise ticket for investigation
5. Offer callback with update

**"My data is slow"**

1. Check data usage and allowance
2. Verify 4G/5G enabled on device
3. Check for throttling if exceeded limit
4. Run speed test if customer willing
5. If persistent, raise network ticket

### Account Changes

**SIM Swap Request**

1. Verify identity (enhanced verification)
2. Confirm reason for swap
3. Check for recent SIM swaps (fraud flag if <30 days)
4. Process swap in system
5. New SIM active within 24 hours
6. Advise customer to keep old SIM until new arrives

**Plan Change Request**

1. Review current plan and usage
2. Recommend suitable alternatives
3. Explain any early upgrade fees
4. Confirm understanding of changes
5. Process change (effective next bill)
6. Send confirmation email

## Complaint Handling

### HEAT Method

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **H** - Hear | Listen to the full complaint |
| **E** - Empathise | Show you understand their frustration |
| **A** - Apologise | Say sorry (doesn't admit liability) |
| **T** - Take Action | Explain how you'll resolve |

### Complaint Categories

| Category | SLA | Escalation |
|----------|-----|------------|
| Billing Dispute | 5 days | Team Lead |
| Service Quality | 10 days | Technical |
| Staff Conduct | 5 days | Manager |
| Contract Issue | 10 days | Legal |

### Escalation Phrases

When customer asks for manager:
> "I understand you'd like to speak with a manager. Let me see if I can resolve this for you first. If not, I'll be happy to escalate."

When you cannot help:
> "I apologise that I'm not able to resolve this directly. I'm going to escalate this to our specialist team who will contact you within [timeframe]."

## Retention (Save Team)

### Cancellation Request Handling

1. **Verify** - Confirm account and intent
2. **Understand** - Ask why they want to leave
3. **Acknowledge** - Show you value them
4. **Offer** - Present retention offer if eligible
5. **Confirm** - Process cancellation if requested

### Retention Offers

| Tenure | Offer Available |
|--------|-----------------|
| <6 months | Limited - standard terms |
| 6-12 months | 10% discount for 6 months |
| 12-24 months | 15% discount OR free add-on |
| 24+ months | 20% discount OR plan upgrade |

### Common Churn Reasons & Responses

**"Too expensive"**
> "I understand budget is important. Let me review your usage to see if there's a more suitable plan that could save you money while keeping you with us."

**"Poor coverage"**
> "I'm sorry to hear you've had coverage issues. Let me check our network status in your area. We've been investing heavily in our network and coverage is improving."

**"Better deal elsewhere"**
> "I appreciate you letting me know. Before you go, let me see what we can offer to match or beat that deal."

## Quality Assurance

### Call Quality Criteria

| Criteria | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Greeting & Verification | 10% |
| Listening & Understanding | 20% |
| Knowledge & Accuracy | 25% |
| Solution & Resolution | 25% |
| Communication & Rapport | 10% |
| Closing & Next Steps | 10% |

### Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Use customer's name
- Summarise understanding
- Offer alternatives
- Document everything
- Follow up when promised

**Don't:**
- Interrupt customer
- Make promises you can't keep
- Use jargon
- Transfer unnecessarily
- Leave customer on hold >2 mins without update

## Regulatory Compliance

### Ofcom Requirements

- Provide clear tariff information
- Honour cancellation rights
- Process complaints within 8 weeks
- Offer ADR after 8 weeks or deadlock
- Accessibility requirements

### GDPR Compliance

- Only access data with legitimate reason
- Don't share data without consent
- Report data breaches immediately
- Right to erasure requests → escalate
