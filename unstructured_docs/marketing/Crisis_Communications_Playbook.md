# SnowTelco Crisis Communications Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook provides guidance for managing communications during crisis situations affecting SnowTelco's reputation, operations, or customers.

## Crisis Classification

### Level 1 - Critical
- Major network outage (>100K customers)
- Data breach affecting customer information
- Regulatory enforcement action
- Executive misconduct
- Fatality involving company operations

**Response:** Full crisis team activation, CEO involvement, hourly updates

### Level 2 - Serious
- Regional network outage (10K-100K customers)
- Service degradation affecting business customers
- Negative national media coverage
- Social media viral incident
- Major customer complaint escalation

**Response:** Crisis team activation, SVP/VP involvement, 2-hour updates

### Level 3 - Moderate
- Local network issues (<10K customers)
- Individual customer complaint in media
- Competitor attack campaign
- Minor data incident

**Response:** Communications team handles, Director involvement, daily updates

## Key Messages Framework

### Template: Network Outage
```
We are aware that some customers in [AREA] are experiencing 
[SERVICE ISSUES]. Our technical teams are working urgently to 
resolve this. We apologize for any inconvenience and will 
provide updates every [X] hours.

Current status: [STATUS]
Estimated resolution: [TIME if known, or "We are working to 
resolve as quickly as possible"]

For updates: [CHANNEL]
```

### Template: Data Incident
```
We have identified [NATURE OF INCIDENT] affecting [SCOPE].
We take the security of customer data extremely seriously.

We have [ACTIONS TAKEN].
We are [ONGOING ACTIONS].

Affected customers will be contacted directly with [DETAILS].
If you have concerns, please contact [SUPPORT CHANNEL].
```

### Template: Service Issue
```
We're aware some customers are experiencing [ISSUE].
This is affecting [SERVICE/AREA/CUSTOMERS].

Our teams are investigating and working to resolve this.
In the meantime, you can [WORKAROUND if applicable].

We apologize for any inconvenience and thank you for your patience.
```

## Media Management

### Authorized Spokespersons
- **CEO:** Major crises, strategic issues
- **COO:** Operational matters
- **VP Communications:** Day-to-day media
- **Subject experts:** Technical briefings only

### Media Guidelines
- Never speculate
- Stick to confirmed facts
- Express empathy for affected customers
- Avoid blame or accusations
- Don't discuss legal matters
- Refer regulatory questions to Comms team

### Social Media Guidelines
- Respond within 15 minutes to direct queries
- Acknowledge issue, don't argue
- Provide factual updates
- Redirect to official channels
- Escalate threats or abuse
- Screenshot and document everything

## Stakeholder Notification

### Priority Order
1. Regulators (Ofcom, ICO if data)
2. Board of Directors
3. Major enterprise customers
4. All employees
5. Partners and suppliers
6. General public

### Regulatory Notification
- Network outages: Ofcom within 24 hours
- Data breaches: ICO within 72 hours
- Safety incidents: HSE immediately

- What could improve

### Report Contents
- Executive summary
- Incident timeline
- Response evaluation
- Customer impact
- Media coverage analysis
- Recommendations

### Metrics to Track
- Time to first response
- Customer complaints received
- Social media sentiment

- Customer churn impact
