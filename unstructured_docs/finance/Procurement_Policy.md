# Procurement Policy
**SnowTelco Communications - Finance & Operations**

## Purpose

This policy establishes the procedures and controls for procurement of goods and services at SnowTelco, ensuring value for money, compliance, and appropriate governance.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All purchases of goods and services
- All employees authorised to procure
- All SnowTelco legal entities

**Exclusions:**
- Payroll and benefits
- Intercompany transactions
- Real estate leases (separate policy)

---

## Procurement Principles

1. **Value for Money** - Best overall value, not just lowest price
2. **Competition** - Competitive process where appropriate
3. **Transparency** - Fair and documented processes
4. **Compliance** - Adherence to laws and regulations
5. **Sustainability** - Environmental and social considerations
6. **Risk Management** - Appropriate due diligence

## Procurement Process

### Step 1: Requisition

**Required Information:**
- Business justification
- Specification/requirements
- Budget code
- Estimated value
- Preferred supplier (if any)
- Required delivery date

### Step 2: Sourcing

| Value | Process Required |
|-------|------------------|
| <£5,000 | Direct purchase (preferred suppliers) |
| £5,000-£25,000 | 3 quotes minimum |
| £25,000-£100,000 | Competitive tender |
| >£100,000 | Formal RFP process |

### Step 3: Evaluation

**Evaluation Criteria (weighted):**
- Price/Total Cost of Ownership (30-40%)
- Quality/Technical fit (25-35%)
- Service/Support (15-20%)
- Supplier capability (10-15%)
- Sustainability (5-10%)

### Step 4: Approval

- Route through approval workflow
- Obtain required signatures
- Budget verification
- Compliance check

### Step 5: Contract/PO

- Issue purchase order
- Execute contract (if required)
- Register with legal
- Set up supplier in system

### Step 6: Receipt and Payment

- Goods receipt/service acceptance
- 3-way match (PO, receipt, invoice)
- Payment per agreed terms
- Supplier performance review

## Competitive Bidding

### When Required

- Purchases >£25,000
- Recurring purchases >£50,000 annually
- Professional services >£25,000
- IT systems >£25,000

### Exemptions (with approval)

| Exemption | Approval Level |
|-----------|----------------|
| Single source (technical) | Director + Procurement |
| Emergency | VP (with retrospective CFO) |
| Strategic partnership | CFO |
| Extension of existing contract | CFO |

### RFP Process

1. Define requirements
2. Identify potential suppliers
3. Issue RFP (minimum 3 weeks response)
4. Conduct Q&A session
5. Evaluate responses
6. Shortlist and presentations
7. Final evaluation and selection
8. Negotiation
9. Award and contract

## Supplier Management

### Supplier Risk Assessment

| Risk Level | Criteria | Review Frequency |
|------------|----------|------------------|
| Critical | >£500k annual, strategic | Quarterly |
| High | £100-500k annual | Semi-annually |
| Medium | £25-100k annual | Annually |
| Low | <£25k annual | As needed |

### Performance Reviews

**Metrics:**
- Quality/defect rate
- Delivery performance
- Price competitiveness
- Service responsiveness
- Innovation

### Exit Strategy

All critical suppliers must have:
- Documented exit plan
- Data return provisions
- Transition support
- Alternative supplier identified

---

## Contract Management

### Standard Terms

- SnowTelco standard terms preferred
- Supplier terms require Legal review
- Payment terms: Net 30 (standard), Net 45 (strategic)
- Liability caps appropriate to risk

### Required Clauses

- Data protection (GDPR compliance)
- Confidentiality
- Security requirements
- Audit rights
- Termination provisions
- Business continuity

### Contract Register

All contracts >£25,000 must be registered:
- Contract management system
- Expiry alerts (90 days)
- Annual review schedule

---

## Sustainability

### Environmental Requirements

- Environmental policy required for suppliers >£100k
- Preference for ISO 14001 certified
- Packaging reduction requirements
- Carbon footprint reporting

### Social Requirements

- Modern slavery statement required
- Living wage commitment
- Diversity and inclusion policy
- Health and safety standards

---

## Compliance

### Prohibited

- Personal purchases
- Splitting orders to avoid thresholds
- Kickbacks or bribes
- Conflicts of interest (undisclosed)
- Circumventing approval process

### Conflict of Interest

Must disclose:
- Financial interest in supplier
- Family members employed by supplier
- Previous employment with supplier
- Gifts or hospitality >£50
